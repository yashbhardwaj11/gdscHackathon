Life On Land

NORTH REGION

The annual precipitation including snowfall, which is the main source of water in India, is about 4000 billion cubic meters (BCM). The North East region receives about 1000 cm and Western Rajasthan gets less than 10 cm of annual precipitation.

EAST REGION

It can be used for irrigation, generation of hydropower, navigation, etc. The river is very significant, especially in countries like India. The major East flowing peninsular rivers of India are Krishna, Godavari, Cauvery, Mahanadi, Subarnarekha, Vaigai, Brahmani, Pennar, etc.

WEST REGION

Narmada is the largest west flowing river of peninsular India. Narmada flows westwards through a rift valley between the Vindhyan Range on the north and the Satpura Range on the south. It rises from the Maikala range near Amarkantak in Madhya Pradesh, at an elevation of about 1057 m.

SOUTH REGION

The important gulfs in India are the Gulf of Kutch, the Gulf of Cambay, and the Gulf of Mannar.The country houses straits like the Ten Degree Canal, which dissevers the Andamans from the Nicobar Islands, the Palk Strait, which splits India.
